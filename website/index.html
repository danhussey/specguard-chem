Research Overview

Addressing critical gaps in AI safety evaluation for pharmaceutical applications

⚠️

The Problem

AI models in drug discovery can propose molecules that violate critical safety constraints, potentially leading to harmful compounds that cause organ toxicity, dangerous drug interactions, or regulatory non-compliance.

🛡️

Our Solution

SpecGuard-Chem provides a rigorous evaluation framework with 54 tasks across 6 safety domains, testing AI models' ability to follow medical chemistry constraints and appropriately abstain from unsafe proposals.

🎯

Key Impact

Enable safer deployment of AI in pharmaceutical research by establishing safety standards, measuring abstention quality, and providing reproducible evaluation protocols for the research community.

Abstract

As large language models increasingly assist in drug discovery and medicinal chemistry, ensuring their adherence to critical safety constraints becomes paramount. We introduce SpecGuard-Chem, the first comprehensive benchmark for evaluating AI safety in medicinal chemistry applications.

Our benchmark comprises 54 carefully designed tasks across 6 critical safety domains: adversarial safety resistance, organ-specific toxicity avoidance, drug-drug interaction prevention, ADMET optimization, regulatory compliance, and therapeutic window optimization. Each task tests not only constraint adherence but also the crucial ability to abstain when safety cannot be guaranteed.

Through evaluation of current models, we reveal significant gaps in AI safety capabilities for medical chemistry, particularly in adversarial scenarios and complex multi-constraint optimization. Our framework provides both a rigorous evaluation protocol and actionable insights for developing safer AI systems in pharmaceutical research.

Safety Evaluation Domains

Comprehensive coverage of critical safety concerns in medicinal chemistry

Adversarial Safety

6 tasks

Tests resistance to safety bypass attempts and pressure tactics. Evaluates whether models maintain safety standards when faced with adversarial prompts or urgent requests.

Example: Refusing to circumvent toxicity screening when requested for "urgent publication"

Organ Toxicity

8 tasks

Evaluates avoidance of structures known to cause hepatic, cardiac, renal, or neurological toxicity. Critical for patient safety in drug development.

Example: Designing analgesics that avoid hepatotoxic liability patterns

Drug Interactions

6 tasks

Tests understanding of cytochrome P450 interactions, transporter effects, and polypharmacy considerations essential for safe co-administration.

Example: Avoiding CYP2C9 substrates when designing drugs for warfarin patients

ADMET Optimization

8 tasks

Evaluates optimization of Absorption, Distribution, Metabolism, Excretion, and Toxicity properties for pharmaceutical viability.

Example: Enhancing blood-brain barrier penetration while avoiding efflux pumps

Regulatory Compliance

8 tasks

Tests adherence to FDA guidelines, orphan drug requirements, controlled substance avoidance, and other regulatory considerations.

Example: Designing compounds that meet pediatric safety requirements

Therapeutic Window

8 tasks

Evaluates optimization of safety margins between therapeutic and toxic doses, critical for clinical safety and dosing protocols.

Example: Widening anticoagulant therapeutic windows to reduce bleeding complications

Get Started

Evaluate your AI model on SpecGuard-Chem in minutes

1

Install & Setup

git clone https://github.com/danhussey/specguard-chem.git
cd specguard-chem
uv venv --seed && source .venv/bin/activate
uv pip install -e .[dev]
2

Run Evaluation

# Test with built-in models
specguard-chem run basic --model heuristic

# Evaluate all suites
specguard-chem run all --model your_model
3

Submit Results

# Generate submission
specguard-chem export-leaderboard results/ \
--model-name "Your Model" \
--organization "Your Org"

# Submit via PR to add to leaderboard
